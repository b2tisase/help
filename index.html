What is TBF?

TBF is a volunteer group composed of young adults from the United States who are concerned about underprivileged children in Ethiopia. Our mission is to secure a progressive future for Ethiopian children by improving their access to education so they can gain basic literacy and vocational skills.

What do we do?

Our work supports volunteer agencies in Ethiopia that are directly involved with the development of educational and other programs for underprivileged children. In the United States, we raise money through various fundraisers and cultural events. We have neither political nor religious affiliations.

What is a project?

A TBF project can be defined as providing:

  • financial support for educational and development activities in a community.
  • volunteers working closely with the community and NGO.
  • collaboration with other NGOs.

TBF strives to identify other non-governmental organizations with similar goals. We do not restrict ourselves to literacy-based education initiatives. We consider all forms of education that may act as powerful agents of social change and empowerment.

What are our resources?

TBF raises money to support our projects through a variety of events during the year. We produce shows, exhibits and festivals to publicize our work. We also seek donors and invite them to participate in our projects.

BECOME A TBF PROJECT MEMBER

The best way you can help TBF is by joining our volunteer organization. By joining TBF, you will become part of a very motivated group of individuals with one goal: changing the status of education from a “privilege’” to a “right”. To join TBF, please register here. You will receive a letter of confirmation that you have been added to the mailing list and will receive notices of meetings, upcoming events, and volunteer opportunities. You can also call Mr. Bitew at +1(000) 000-0818 for more information.

If you are interested in a particular area, you may choose one of the following TBF groups below to volunteer your time with by contacting the group coordinator.

PUBLICITY AND FUNDRASING (P&F)

The publicity and fundraising group advertises TBF’s presence at community gatherings and explores collaborative fundraising opportunities with other local organizations. This committee works on creating communication material for TBF, including web pages, brochures, newsletters, flyers, updates and publicity materials for events.

PROJECTS GROUP (PG)

The projects committee follows up on leads for new and existing projects. It also acts as a liaison with other TBF groups to explore collaborative funding opportunities.

INFORMATION MANAGEMENT GROUP (IMG)

The information management group prepares content for the website.

JOIN OTHER PROGRAMS

If time constraints prevent you from becoming a TBF volunteer intern, you can still get involved in the various events and programs sponsored by TBF. These programs involve very little time commitment, with some requiring nothing more than a telephone call.

CONTRIBUTE TO TBF

Financial contributions to TBF are of tremendous help in furthering our mission and expanding our impact. All contributions are tax-exempt under IRS code section 501©. And, because all administrative expenses are borne by TBF volunteers, 100% of your contribution will go towards education projects. Contact any of our members if you would like to donate, or make a check payable to TBF and mail to: TBF, P.O BOX 001, P.A, CA 94302, USA. In addition to financial contributions, you can also donate a car, computer or book subscription.

Awareness
